### Vedic

{{<poet>}} nā́sad āsīn nó sád āsīt tadā́nīṃ
nā́sīd rájo nó víomā paró yát
kím ā́varīvaḥ kúha kásya śármann
ámbhaḥ kím āsīd gáhanaṃ gabhīrám

ná mr̥tyúr āsīd amŕ̥taṃ ná tárhi
ná rā́triyā áhna āsīt praketáḥ
ā́nīd avātáṃ svadháyā tád ékaṃ
tásmād dhānyán ná paráḥ kíṃ canā́sa

táma āsīt támasā gūháḷam ágre
apraketáṃ saliláṃ sárvam ā idám
tuchyénābhú ápihitaṃ yád ā́sīt
tápasas tán mahinā́jāyataíkam

kā́mas tád ágre sám avartatā́dhi
mánaso rétaḥ prathamáṃ yád ā́sīt
sató bándhum ásati nír avindan
hr̥dí pratī́ṣyā kaváyo manīṣā́

tiraścī́no vítato raśmír eṣām
adháḥ svid āsī́d upári svid āsīt
retodhā́ āsan mahimā́na āsan
svadhā́ avástāt práyatiḥ parástāt

kó addhā́ veda ká ihá prá vocat
kúta ā́jātā kúta iyáṃ vísr̥ṣṭiḥ
arvā́g devā́ asyá visárjanena
áthā kó veda yáta ābabhū́va

iyáṃ vísr̥ṣṭir yáta ābabhū́va
yádi vā dadhé yádi vā ná
yó asyā́dhyakṣaḥ paramé vyoman
só aṅgá veda yádi vā ná véda{{</poet>}}

### English

{{<poet>}}There was neither non-existent nor existent then,
neither darkness nor the further heaven,
What moved? Where? In whose protection?
Was it water, deep and profound?

There was neither death nor immortality then,
neither night nor day had their appearance.
That ONE breathed breathlessly with its own power;
There was nothing except it.

Darkness was at first engulfed by darkness,
all this was undifferentiated surging waters.
When it was formless and covered by the void,
by the power of heat was the One born.

Desire arose over in the beginning,
that was the first seed of mind.
Relation of the existent in the non-existent did the wise
with wisdom found out searching in their hearts.

Their cord stretched transversely,
was it down below or was it above?
There were the seed-bearers, the great ones,
ethos was down below and offering further away.

Who knows for sure? Who will proclaim it here?
Whence was it born? Whence is this creation?
The gods were after its creation,
who knows who from where it arose?

From where did this creation arose,
did he place it or did he not?
He who supervises it from the highest heaven,
surely, he knows. Or does he?{{</poet>}}
